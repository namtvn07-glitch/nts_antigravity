# Generation DNA: Cozy

# I. VISUAL DNA
## Consistency Rules
All assets must feel unified through a shared soft vector aesthetic, thick consistent outlines, and lack of sharp geometric corners. 
## Shape Language
Extremely round, soft edges, and chunky vector silhouettes. Use sweeping curves and avoid sharp angles or rigid straight lines.
## Color Script (Rarity)
- **Common:** Warm pastels, earthy tones (browns, soft greens, sandy yellows).
- **Epic/Rare:** High saturation, glowing effects (e.g., magenta pinks, bright gold) contrasting against the base palette.
## Material Polish
Flat shaded surfaces with subtle, soft gradients. No photorealistic textures; rely on bold, stylized color blocking and thick dark strokes for definition. Wood and thatch are common UI materials.

# II. PRODUCT LOGIC
## Readability (3-Second Rule)
Assets must instantly communicate their function. Exaggerate proportions (like big heads on characters or oversized UI buttons) to ensure immediate recognition on small screens.
## Progression Logic
Visual upgrades should introduce warmer, more saturated colors or distinct glowing effects (e.g., standard egg vs. glowing pink egg vs. golden egg).
## The Juice (Animation states)
Key elements should have dynamic visual feedback—buttons pop with bright yellow highlights, and rare items emit magical glows.
## UI Interaction rules
Use clear visual hierarchy. Actionable elements (like "SPIN" buttons) must use high-contrast, salient colors (bright yellow) and distinct shapes to separate them from static backgrounds.

# III. TECHNICAL EXCELLENCE
## Mesh / Topology rules
Keep vector paths simple and optimized. Outline thickness must remain consistent across different scales of props and characters.
## Texel Density
Maintain uniform resolution across 2D sprites to avoid blurry edges on UI elements vs crisp characters. 
## Modular design / Recolor limits
Ensure base sprites can be easily hue-shifted for rarity without breaking the thick black outlines. Characters should be front-facing or 3/4 view to allow modular accessory swapping (like hats/clothes).

# IV. GAMEPLAY AFFORDANCE & DESIGN LOGIC
## Silhouette rules
Strong, simple silhouettes. A character's head should be up to 50% of the body mass to emphasize expression. 
## Guiding lines
Use soft, organic curves (like shorelines or winding paths) to direct the player's eye naturally. 
## Affordance logic
Highly salient, glowing, or contrasting colors indicate primary interactables and rewards. Dangerous or inactive elements should blend more with the earthy background tones. Depth contrast should consistently separate light foregrounds from darker backgrounds.
